Hire smarter, not harder.

Qwick Jobs extends our pre-vetted Pro network into permanent (FTE & PTE) hospitality hiring. Post-for-free, pay-on-success: $1,500 promotional fee per hire (vs. $2,500 TOS baseline), backed by a 30-day guarantee. The pitch is simple — replace stacks of self-reported resumes with objective shift history, an AI Match score, and a paid working interview.

Demo Playbook

7 steps for a Jobs conversation

Pull this up before any Sales or AM call. Adapted from the Walkthrough v3.

1. Lead with free, not with $1,500.
Open by offering to ingest their job postings via the crawler. Trey: "If a business has an active job listing, why can't you walk away from that conversation with an account created and jobs posted?"
2. Frame the problem.
Hiring through Indeed costs time and dollars, and most hires churn inside 90 days. You're already trying to hire Pros off our platform — let's make that an actual workflow.
3. Walk the business surface.
Show the job-posting flow, the matched-applicant view, the MatchScore shortlist, and the AI summary on each applicant.
4. The working interview.
Show how a temp shift becomes a try-out without breaking their existing workflow. Adam: "The problem was you had to hire them just to fire them three days later."
5. Drop the anchors.
$1,500 vs. their current Indeed cost-per-hire. 30-day guarantee. $5/hr buy-down on prior worked hours. One credit covers multiple guarantee rounds.
6. Pivot in dead conversations.
Danny: "Hey, we're trying to phase out temp spend — that conversation usually is dead in the water. This allows you to pivot."
7. Close on a single hire.
"Let's land one good hire together. If it doesn't stick, the credit comes back."
FAQ

Common questions — answered

Pulled from the May 14 Internal FAQ v3. Use word-for-word when partners or teammates ask.

What does it cost a business to hire through Jobs?

$1,500 per successful hire. Posting is free. There's no fee for receiving applicants, sending working interviews (those bill as normal marketplace shifts), or messaging candidates.

When exactly is the $1,500 charged?

When the business sends an offer, the card is charged and the credit moves into an "in-use" state. The credit is deducted (revenue recognized) only when the Pro clicks Accept. If the Pro declines or no-shows, the same credit can be redirected to another applicant or even a different job posting at the same location.

How does the buy-down work?

$5 per hour off the fee for prior worked hours with that specific business, capped at 300 hours. It's a burn-down, not a cliff — 150 hours of prior work knocks $750 off the fee.

What exactly does the 30-day guarantee cover?

If a hire doesn't stick within 30 days, the credit is returned and the business can restart the search at no additional cost. Default behavior is credit-back, not cash refund. One credit can cover multiple replacement rounds — Qwick keeps shift margin on every working-interview shift along the way.

What's a working interview?

A temp shift offered to a job applicant so the business can try them out before formally hiring. Structured, paid, and a replacement for the unpaid "stash" tryouts common in hospitality. Trey: "It's kind of like dating. Better to find out up front than after onboarding to ADP."

What if a Pro accepts a working interview and then declines the offer?

Today: no pro-side penalty. The business retains its credit (Pro didn't accept), the working-interview shift was already paid at marketplace rates, and the business can pivot the credit to another applicant. Known gap — don't oversell Pro commitment.

Which markets are we launching in?

Hybrid (1099 + W2) and 1099-only markets are in. W-2-only markets are out for now while joint-employment risk is evaluated — that excludes the West Coast, Denver, and Chicago at launch.

How are MSA / restricted accounts (Aramark, Boeing) handled?

Owned by Partnerships (Courtney Gulseth, Jared Gidan, Ed Gurrieri). Business-side blocking is in place. Pro-side blocking isn't built yet — a manager at an MSA account who happens to have a Pro account could still see Jobs in the Pro app. Don't pitch Jobs to an MSA without checking first.

Won't businesses just hire Pros off-platform to avoid the fee?

Some will try. This is already happening at scale today and we're capturing zero revenue from it. The goal isn't policing — it's pricing the workflow attractively enough that businesses choose to come back into it. The 30-day guarantee plus curated matching + AI screening are the anchors. ToS updates reinforce that hires belong on-platform.

Won't this pluck our best Pros out of the temp marketplace?

Real concern under active monitoring. Counter: top Pros are already getting hired off-platform behind our backs; Pros opt in to full-time work and are notified when a shift is a working interview; Qwick keeps shift margin on every working interview; future V3 matching will balance supply protection vs. revenue capture.

Easy entry point for sales conversations?

Lead with free, not with $1,500. Grab their job posting URL, drop it on their business profile, and our crawler auto-posts. Free syndication into our Pro pool with zero commitment. Danny: "Hey, we're trying to phase out temp spend — that conversation usually is dead in the water. This allows you to pivot."

What positions does Jobs support today?

An expanded position list compared to the marketplace — F&B managers explicitly included (major customer pain point). Working-interview-incompatible roles like security guards are excluded. Director/manager placements ($60k–$100k+) are roadmap.
